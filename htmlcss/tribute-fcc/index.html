Linus Torvalds

  • 1969: Born on December 28 in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1981: Began programming on a Commodore VIC-20 at age 11.
  • 1987: Purchased his first PC, an Intel 80386-based machine.
  • 1988: Enrolled at the University of Helsinki to study Computer Science.
  • 1991: Released the first version of the Linux kernel on September 17.
  • 1992: Linux adopted the GNU General Public License, making it open-source.
  • 1996: The first official mascot of Linux, Tux the Penguin, was introduced.
  • 1997: Published his autobiography, Just for Fun, co-written with David Diamond.
  • 1999: Received the EFF Pioneer Award for his contributions to open-source software.
  • 2003: Created Git, a distributed version control system, which was released in 2005.
  • 2012: Awarded the Millennium Technology Prize for his creation of Linux.
  • 2023: Continues to work on the Linux kernel, overseeing its development.
